<td>What is it for? To name the root causes that block Effective Data Science so you can de-risk and design interventions (UDS, EPS, EOP). Why important? Approach 2: success = managing failure risks first; mapping blockers directs effort to causes.</td>
<td>Ultimate Blockers of Effective Data Science = the root systems (psychological, process, environmental) that make data science unsustainable, inefficient, or non-scalable—e.g. unclear problem framing, irreproducibility, undocumented assumptions, no validation loop, incentive misalignment, context collapse.</td>
<td>You name one blocker at a time (e.g. Blocker #1), trace what drives it (UBS.UD) and what weakens it (UBS.UB), then design principles/tools/steps.</td>
<td>UDS: drive to master the domain + hierarchical questioning + support system (templates, Agent, Learning Book) so mapping is low-friction.</td>
<td>Deep questioning forces you to separate cause vs symptom; the template forces you to fill drivers/blockers/principles so the map is complete.</td>
<td>Hierarchy of Science (trace to governing layer); Approach 2 (de-risk first); active recall and Socratic Q&A.</td>
<td>ILE (one workspace: conversation + structural Markdown), templates (questions × components), Agent (world-class professional, clarifying questions, possibilities).</td>
<td>Local-first Markdown repo; level-appropriate entry points (A); time to contemplate and choose (no append without Learner approval).</td>
<td>You treat a symptom as the blocker (e.g. "document more") and never name the cause (e.g. no incentive to document).</td>
<td>UBS: manual paste / context collapse; UBS.UD: fast system (good-enough, no time to map).</td>
